## Conversation

### Session: 2026-05-18T10:30:00-04:00

**Human:** I keep having useful conversations with AI, but they disappear into separate apps. What is the real problem here?

**AI:** The problem is not only storage. It is continuity. When your conversations stay inside closed platforms, your thinking cannot accumulate across time, tools, and models.

**Human:** So this is about memory?

**AI:** Yes, but not memory as a platform feature. It is memory as something you own. A local markdown file can be read by you, searched by tools, backed up, published, or used as context for a future model.

### Key Turning Point: From chat log to thought asset

**Human:** That changes the category. A conversation is not just a transcript. It is part of the thinking process.

**AI:** Exactly. LocalThink Format treats a conversation as a thought asset: something that can be preserved, extended, connected, and returned to.

**Human:** Then the format has to stay simple.

**AI:** Simple enough for anyone to write, structured enough for tools to understand, and open enough that no platform controls it.
